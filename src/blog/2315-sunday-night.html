--- title: 2315, sunday night, 21st of june date: 2026-21-06 ---

it’s 2315 on a sunday night. you're just winding down for the night. you have work at 8, and you're already in relatively insignificant but prolonged sleep debt. you're doing your nails after an evening of linux, laundry, sudoku and testing out a new mouse you bought. you're impressed with how this whole vyvanse thing has worked out. you've said to multiple people that you've run out of stuff to do, and that's not true. you always find more things to do. things you've been putting off. small enhancements to your life in one way or another. you restored the media server that promptly shat itself after 2 months of uptime on a shitty sd card, ported it over not once, but twice to a better sd card and finally an emmc chip. you've been vacuuming and cleaning pretty much weekly. you're carving through the other tidbits you've been putting off, mostly tech projects, some blog posts, a book you're reading. it's going well. but there's this gnawing feeling holding you every time you find something else to do, buy something you've meant to buy. that sort of feeling that nothing is ever going to be enough, that you're just burdening yourself with workload to avoid sitting in silence, or worse, instagram reels. and as you're jotting down the list for the next day, contemplating what you're going to bring up in therapy, lamenting how weeks go by quicker now you work full time, it hits you.

i only want to focus on music from now on. i just want to focus on music.

you've reached a plateau, a state where the only things left in your list are small tasks that take 5 minutes. buy some command strips. hang a poster. clean out your email inbox. things that can't be ‘the thing you are doing today'. you can't keep delaying yourself with the small things. you can't keep checking and looking at the things you've already done. the media server is running. you've already made backups of everything on there, including the os. the blog hasn't changed, you should know, the only one who can change it is you. yes honey, your everyday-carry backpack is very impressive. stop fucking stalling.

you know, you've always kinda shifted the goal posts with the thing you've said your life is about. first, it was that you didn't have time, then it was your mental health, not having some of your core needs met, not having stability, being frustrated with your shitty gear, not being medicated. but now? now you have a much better setup, stability, your responsibilities are being met, you have the medication that makes you a fucking powerhouse. now you just aren't doing the thing you claim you want to. donald glover had a tidbit in some interview, deriding those who ask why they couldn't do what he does with the answer that they don't want to do it. you love donald glover. you hate that answer. not because you think it's wrong, but because you've always ascribed some moral failing on your end for that holding true. you want to make music. you don't want to do it though. not because you don't enjoy it, not because you're a fucking poser. you know what you are. it’s because you don't want to face the reality that your skill doesn't allow you to fabricate magic out of thin air. you know how to do all the things, but not at a stage you feel confident it reflects your brain.

but to speak on that, you've recently felt more and more that the medication for adhd has made your autism pop out. you heard it was a common thing. you hate how it's manifested. you're more awkward, twitchy, talkative, mask less. you don't hate yourself, you know yourself enough to like what it is you are, but you hate how you're now being filtered. autism dysphoria. fucking sucks. but! you declare - if the purpose of art is nothing else but to express oneself and invite others to see the world through your eyes, would this not be a better time than ever to utilise it? to show that you're not just some autistic fucking nerd obsessed with technology, but a motherfucker who overomanticises anything that moves? that you've got the spirit of a hustler and the swagger of a college kid? you'll take any reason you can get. it’s best not to overthink it i suppose. you just know the following.

i want to focus on music from now on. i've met my needs, i'm in a good spot, i've got my other hobbies, they exist, but i just want to focus on music from now on.
